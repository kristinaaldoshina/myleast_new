Modern learning paradigm states that all participants of a learning process (learner, teacher, parents/relatives, etc.) have to encourage the learner to create and lead his own learning process. Modern society aknowledges, that people learn differently, and those differences depend on many factors, like:

  • age,
  • previous experiences,
  • physical environment,
  • social environment,
  • emotional state,
  • physiology,
  • and etc.

Influence of these factors forms person's learning style - the totality of various human qualities and environmental factors that stimulate (or help) a person to concentrate on learning. It means, that if a learner knows his learning style, he can learn in the most efficient way and achieve stunning results. There is no the best learning style - each one has advantages.

Nowadays many different learning theories suggest various classifications of learning styles (click on "Nature of Learning" below to find out more). We suggest you to concentrate on Honey-Mumford's and Fleming's theories, which are easy to understand and easy to apply by any participant of a learning process.

In 1986 Peter Honey and Alan Mumford identified four distinct learning styles, based on at which stage a learner starts his learning process (see picture below). You can find out more by clicking on "Honey and Mumford Learning Styles" below.

In 1987 Neil Fleming defined three learning styles, based on what modality preferences (visual, auditory or kinaesthetic) dominate in a learner's communication. You can find out more by clicking on "Fleming's VAK Learning Styles" below.

It is newer to late to become a successful learner: just complete HM test and VAK tests to find out your preferable learning style(s) and recommendations for efficient learning!

Usually a person has no a single dominant learning style, that is why after completing tests you will see distributions of how strongly each style appears in you.

Please note, that both tests have no option to save your choices to continue later, but you can download results (descriptions) after a test is completed.
